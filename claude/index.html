01

The world runs on lies.

The internet was built on trust.
Not real trust — just the illusion of it.

Email assumes you are who you say you are.
Phone numbers assume you belong to the device.
Video calls assume the voice is real.

None of it was designed to be secure.
We just never stopped to ask:
Who's actually on the other side?

02

Deepfakes didn't break the system.

They exposed it.

The protocols holding up our digital world — TCP/IP, SMTP, DNS, SS7 — were built decades ago.

No encryption. No identity. No verification.

Now, AI doesn't need to hack you.
It just needs to sound like you.

One in ten people have already received an AI voice clone.
77% lost money because of it. — McAfee, 2024
03

People are the new attack surface.

Scammers don't exploit firewalls anymore.
They exploit emotion, trust, and panic.

45% of people say they'd respond to a fake call or message from a loved one asking for money.

Technology made us reachable.
AI made us impersonable.
And no one built a defense for human trust.

04

Enter realxreal.ai

A mobile app that lets you authenticate who's really calling, emailing, or meeting you — before you ever respond.

We combine cryptography, biometrics, and a new class of human-centered verification called the Doppelganger Protocol.

No cloud uploads.
No third-party servers.
No "trust us."
Just you, your device, and the truth.
